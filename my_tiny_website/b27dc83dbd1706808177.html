Kelsey V. Bailey is a UX/UI designer who has a diverse background and brings that diversity into everything she does. After teaching English for many years, she enrolled in a UX/UI Design bootcamp to transfer her communication and people-centered skills to a career in design, where she could exercise her creativity to find unique solutions for human problems.

One of her recent projects included collaborating in teams to redesign the website of a talent recruitment agency. Kelsey conducted internal stakeholder interviews, analyzed user data, and created information architecture. She took a lead role in conducting weekly online meetings and produced low/high fidelity wireframes and usable prototypes. Kelsey fulfilled all the changes informed by usability testing and presented the entire project to the jurors. Their design was selected as a runner-up to be implemented as a collaboration with the winners.

In addition to her experience as a UX/UI designer, Kelsey has also worked as a freelance web designer, directly collaborating with a team of psychologists in Barcelona to design an informative website for their new school. She delivered high-fidelity designs and created original illustrations based on their request for a somber tone. Kelsey implemented these designs in WordPress using both the site editor and CSS and HTML.

Kelsey's experience also includes teaching English as a second language, both to professionals and children/teens in Barcelona and Northern Italy. She created lessons using authentic materials and facilitated conversations among students based on their needs and relevant interests. Additionally, she has worked as a photographer and laboratory assistant at the Natural History Museum of Los Angeles, where she photographed museum specimens, managed image inventory, and aided in biodiversity research projects. Kelsey also led public demonstrations for museum visitors using a projection of insect samples under a microscope.

Kelsey's educational background includes completing the All Women UX/UI Design Bootcamp, where she focused on design thinking, human-centered design, wireframing, and prototyping. Her final project was selected to be featured on AllWomen's online archives. She also holds a Bachelor of Arts degree from the University of Southern California, majoring in Political Science and minoring in Photography and Social Change, graduating cum laude.

Kelsey possesses a wide range of design skills, including user experience, interaction design, user interface, prototyping, visual design, wireframing, responsive design, low/high fidelity mockups, user flows, graphic design, illustration, mobile app, web design, and research (user interviews, user testing, information architecture, journey mapping, quantitative analysis, A/B testing, competitor analysis, design strategy). She is proficient in software such as Figma, Illustrator, Photoshop, WordPress, Webflow, Adobe XD, HTML, CSS, and Miro.

Apart from her design skills, Kelsey also excels in rapid iteration, cross-functional collaboration and communication, problem-solving, adaptability, project management, and a human-centered approach. She has additional skills in photography, ceramics, presentation, Spanish (proficient), Vietnamese (conversational).
